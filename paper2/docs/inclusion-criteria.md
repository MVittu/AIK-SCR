# Inclusion criteria

## Population

Include studies involving healthy participants, athletes, exercisers, trained participants, recreationally active participants, sport students, or healthy volunteers.

Include studies with mixed samples only when data for healthy, athletic, trained, or physically active participants are reported separately or when the sample is not primarily clinical.

## Concept / intervention

Include studies examining a breathing intervention, breathing maneuver, or breathing-related exposure relevant to sport, exercise, or performance.

Eligible breathing strategies include, but are not limited to:

* breath holding, breath-hold training, apnea, or apnoea;
* voluntary hypoventilation or reduced-frequency breathing during exercise;
* voluntary hyperventilation, hypocapnic hyperventilation, or deep/rapid breathing intended to alter ventilation;
* paced breathing, slow-paced breathing, coherent breathing, resonance breathing, metronome breathing, or controlled breathing;
* diaphragmatic or abdominal breathing;
* pranayama, yogic breathing, Bhramari breathing, alternate-nostril breathing, uni-nostril breathing, or nostril breathing;
* Wim Hof-type breathing;
* box breathing, hook breathing, or other structured breath-regulation practices;
* HRV biofeedback or respiratory biofeedback only when the biofeedback is used to guide, monitor, or train breathing rather than acting as the main physiological intervention.

## Context

Include studies conducted in sport, exercise, training, recovery, competition, or performance-relevant laboratory contexts.

Eligible contexts include sport-specific tasks, exercise-performance tests, physical-performance tasks, recovery between exercise bouts, psychophysiological preparation for performance, and laboratory tasks with plausible sport or physical-performance relevance.

## Outcomes

Include studies reporting at least one sport-, exercise-, or physical-performance-relevant outcome, such as endurance, sprint performance, time-trial performance, repeated-sprint ability, power, strength, jump performance, swimming/cycling/running performance, skill accuracy, shooting performance, reaction time, balance, fatigue resistance, recovery performance, or other objective or validated performance-related outcomes.

Physiological, psychological, perceptual, feasibility, adherence, or safety outcomes may be included as secondary outcomes when the study also has a sport-, exercise-, or performance-relevant context.

## Study design

Include original empirical studies of any design, including randomized controlled trials, crossover studies, non-randomized experimental studies, before-after studies, observational studies, and pilot studies.

For a scoping review, do not exclude solely because the study is small, acute, non-randomized, or exploratory, provided it meets the population, concept, context, and outcome criteria.

## Publication characteristics

Include peer-reviewed journal articles. Include theses, dissertations, conference papers, or grey literature only if the review protocol explicitly allows grey literature.
