# Exclusion criteria / Rayyan exclusion reasons

## Exclude: Review or non-original article

Exclude reviews, systematic reviews, scoping reviews, meta-analyses, narrative reviews, editorials, commentaries, letters without original data, opinion pieces, protocols without results, and theoretical papers.

## Exclude: Wrong population

Exclude studies conducted primarily in clinical or patient populations, unless a healthy, athletic, trained, recreationally active, or physically active subgroup is reported separately.

Examples of excluded clinical populations include COPD, asthma, sleep apnea, stroke, pulmonary disease, dyspnea-related conditions, cardiac disease, neurological disease, or rehabilitation-only samples.

## Exclude: Wrong intervention / exposure

Exclude studies that do not test or observe a breathing maneuver, breathing intervention, or breath-regulation strategy.

Exclude respiratory muscle training, inspiratory muscle training, expiratory muscle training, respiratory loading, resisted breathing, pressure-threshold loading, aided inspiration, oxygen supplementation, hypoxic chambers, altitude tents, altered gas mixtures, or other device-based interventions where the device or altered gas condition is the main intervention rather than self-regulated breathing.

Exclude meditation, relaxation, mindfulness, yoga, or biofeedback interventions when the breathing component is absent, not described, or cannot be separated from a broader multicomponent intervention.

## Exclude: Wrong context

Exclude studies with no sport, exercise, physical-performance, recovery, competition, or performance-relevant context.

Exclude studies focused only on general health, stress, anxiety, sleep, cognition, education, occupational performance, or clinical rehabilitation unless they include a clear exercise, sport, or physical-performance task.

## Exclude: Wrong outcome

Exclude studies that report only physiological, psychological, autonomic, biochemical, or perceptual outcomes without any sport-, exercise-, recovery-, or physical-performance-relevant outcome.

Examples of insufficient outcomes when reported alone include HRV, blood pressure, oxygen saturation, end-tidal CO2, lactate, cortisol, anxiety, mood, relaxation, perceived stress, or respiratory parameters without a performance-relevant task or outcome.

## Exclude: Wrong publication type

Exclude abstracts without sufficient data, protocols, unavailable full texts, duplicate records, animal studies, non-human studies, and non-English articles if language restriction is applied.

## Exclude: Duplicate

Exclude duplicate records, secondary reports of the same study, or records superseded by a more complete publication. Retain the most complete report.
