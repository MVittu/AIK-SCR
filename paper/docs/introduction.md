Introduction
Optimal respiratory strategies represent a physiological mechanism capable of modulating the autonomic nervous system, psychological stress, and physical execution in competitive sports (Migliaccio2023 - https://doi.org/10.3390/sports11050103). Despite increased integration into athletic training, the categorization and validation of breathing techniques (BTs) performed without external devices remain fragmented and lack standardized empirical protocols (Laborde2022 - https://doi.org/10.1080/1750984X.2022.2145573). A systematic evaluation of existing literature is required to consolidate empirical evidence on their efficacy. The primary objectives of this scoping review are to map the literature on breathing techniques (BTs) executed without external devices, and evaluate their effects on sports performance.
